Proper Disposal of Plastic Wastes

by: Maui Adrian R. Ortega & Karen Angela B. Lopez

    We, students of Bulacan State University, have been given rules that we have to follow, with regards to waste segregation and disposal. It is saddening to say that based on our observation from the time we have spent in our school, we have noticed that barely any student abide these rules.


    At first, we were given the privilege of different trash bins for the different kinds of wastes (Biodegradable, Non-biodegradable and Recyclable), but it was changed to a single trash bin due to the majority of the students neglecting the proper way of disposal. There was also a movement called the “No Plastic Policy” that has been implemented by the school long ago, but the stores that sell inside the school premises do not abide by theses rules. Though, the school has promoted the “Bring your own tumbler” movement, they only removed plastic bottles that is filled with water, but not other beverages that is contained in plastic bottles. Plastic utensils still populate the school premises even though these has been long banned by the school and plastic wastes brought from the outside still end up being thrown inside the school premises. Overall, plastic wastes still exist inside the school. Though there may have been a lot of movements and policies implemented, the amount of plastic wastes garnered still depends on the willingness of an individual to follow these rules. Without their cooperation, our school would end up being filled with plastic wastes.


    The same can be said in our country, the Philippines. The majority of the citizens do not throw their garbage in the proper disposals. They usually just throw them away, rather than finding a trash bin and disposing it properly. This results to the vast amounts of wastes that can be found in various cities, be it rural or urban, and the huge quantities of wastes that can be seen in our environment. This causes the loss of the natural aesthetic charm of our environment. Aside from the environment losing its aesthetic charm, wastes scattered also makes our cities prone to disasters such as floods and diseases like dengue and leptospirosis. All of these can be prevented, only if the citizens follow the proper ways of wastes disposal.
